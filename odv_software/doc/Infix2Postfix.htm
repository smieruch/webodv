1         General Information

Infix2Postfix is an application for the conversion of mathematical expressions from so-called Infix Notation to Postfix Notation. Infix notation is the common arithmetic and logical formula notation, in which operators are written infix-style between the operands they act on (e.g. 2 + 2), while postfix notation is a mathematical notation wherein every operator follows all of its operands. Postfix notation (also called Reverse Polish notation) is needed in Ocean Data View (ODV) expressions and macros.

 

Figure 1‑1: The Infix2Postfix application window.

The normal way of using Infix2Postfix is to enter an infix expression in the upper text field (see Figure 1-1). Pressing the Translate button will convert the infix expressing and will put the corresponding postfix notation in the lower text field. Pressing the Copy to Clipboard button will place the postfix expression on the system’s clipboard, then allowing pasting it into the ODV Expression and Macro edit dialogs. Pressing Help will display this document.

1.1         Infix Expressions

In infix expressions you can use the operators + - * / ** (^ may be used instead of **) for the basic arithmetic operations, sqrt for the square root, ln and exp for the natural logarithm and exponential, log for the base-10 logarithm, sin and cos for sine and cosine (arguments in radians), abs for the absolute value, and int, floor, ceil for integer parts of the argument. Use min and max for the minimum and maximum of two operands.

As operands you use numbers or terms of the form #1, #2, …, #9 to reference the values of the input variables 1 up to 9. Use the %H for the elevation at the station position (given in meters above sea level; positive for land and negative for ocean), %t  for the time of observation (in years since 1900 calculated from station date and time metadata; e.g., %t=84.4877 for June 27 1984), %d for the day of the year  (also calculated from station date metadata), %x  for the (east-) longitude and %y for the (north-) latitude, respectively. Note that the elevation variable %H is only available if the GEBCO1 and/or ETOPO2 optional packages are installed. Intermediate stored results can be accessed using the %0, %1, ..., %9 codes. This is explained further in the examples below.
